# Introduction

Algebraic K-theory is a branch of mathematics that studies algebraic structures with a ring representation using techniques from homological algebra. conceptually it can be seen as the study of certain kinds of invariants of matrices over a ring but its main interests resides in its applications on fields such as:
- Algebraic geometry
- Algebraic topology
- Operator theory
- High energy physics
- ...

Our interest resides in its applications to algebraic topology and as such this post will have definitios and techniques meant to be used in that field even if they are generic enough to be interesting even if topology is not the intended application.

# The projective class group.
We start defining $K_0(R)$, the projective class group of a ring $R$, and studing its properties and how to calculate it.

## Definitions

> **Definition.**
> Let $R$ be a ring. We define $K_0(R)$ as the abelian group generated by the isomorphism classes of finitely generated projective $R$-modules and with the group operation defined as follows:
>
> for every short exact sequence of finitely generated projective $R$-modules
> $$0 \to P_0 \to P_1 \to P_2 \to 0$$
> we have the relation $[P_1] = [P_0] + [P_2]$.

This definition is a bit abstract but it can be seen as a generalization of the notion of dimension of a vector space. In fact, if $R$ is a field, then every finitely generated projective $R$-module is free and thus isomorphic to $R^n$ for some $n \in \mathbb{N}$ and we have that $K_0(R) \cong \mathbb{Z}$ with the isomorphism given by $[R^n] \mapsto n$.
